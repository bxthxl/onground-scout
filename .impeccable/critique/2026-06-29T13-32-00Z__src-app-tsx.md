## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**Does this look AI-generated? Yes  -  immediately**, and the damning part is it trips the exact tells PRODUCT.md forbids:

- **Cream/sand default background**  -  `--paper #faf7f2` + radial gold glow (index.css:2,14). Anti-reference #1.
- **Uppercase tracked eyebrow on every section**  -  `.eyebrow` appears 4x (App.tsx:165, 206, 222, 266).
- **Twin identical card grids**  -  4 benefit cards (App.tsx:191-202) AND Basic/Premium packages with byte-identical feature lists except visit count (App.tsx:51-58 vs 64-71).
- **Ghost-cards**  -  1px border + box-shadow 0 18px 45px on .step/.package-card/.request-form (App.css:291-294); .field-note repeats it.
- **Over-rounding**  -  `--radius: 18px` exceeds the 16px card ceiling.
- **Uniform heavy weight**  -  everything 750 - 900; "premium" needs weight *contrast*, not uniform bold.

Earned/acceptable: numbered process circles (real 3-step sequence), single glass header. No gradient text, no neon.

**Deterministic scan:** `detect.mjs --json src/App.tsx` → `[]`, exit 0 (clean). **But low-reach:** styles live in external App.css (out of scan scope), so all computed-value rules (low-contrast, gray-on-color, dark-glow, tight-leading, oversized-h1, nested-cards) had no input. ## Overall Impression

A competent, fast-loading page with a genuinely good spine  -  specific hero promise, fear-naming copy, transparent pricing  -  that **drops trust at exactly the moment it must hold**: the form and the post-submit success message. For a scam-fearful, ESL, mobile audience, the page asserts trust but never *shows* it (no faces, no vetting, no sample report, no payment-safety line), and it leaks developer jargon ("Supabase", "the owner") into the highest-stakes copy. The single biggest opportunity: rebuild the bottom third around proof and reassurance, and scrub every system term.

## What's Working

1. **Transparent, upfront pricing** ($150/$250 + add-ons before any form)  -  the strongest anti-scam signal on the page; enacts "show the work."
2. **Plain-language, fear-naming copy**  -  "before you send money or sign documents" / "not after the keys are collected" speaks the user's actual fear in ESL-friendly sentences.
3. **Low-commitment human on-ramp + a11y basics**  -  WhatsApp/email contact, autoComplete, prefers-reduced-motion block, visible focus ring, labelled inputs, role=alert/status.

## Priority Issues

**[P0] System/dev jargon at the trust-critical moments.** "Supabase" / "environment values" / "the owner" appear in body copy (App.tsx:269), form heading (267), and the success message (143-144). *Why it matters:* for a scam-fear ESL audience this is the most damaging possible signal  -  it reads amateur and fly-by-night, the worst failure per the brief. *Fix:* rewrite to human, reassuring language ("Send your details and a local scout reaches out within 24 hours. We never handle your rent money."); remove every system term from user-facing copy. *Command:* `clarify`

**[P0] No evidence  -  the page claims trust but never shows it.** No scout faces, no vetting explanation, no testimonials, no sample report/checklist, no refund/payment-safety statement. *Why it matters:* the brief demands proof over adjectives for an audience that's been burned; "people-powered, warmth" is absent  -  not one human face. *Fix:* add a scout-vetting section, real visit photos, a visible sample report, a testimonial, and a payment-safety line at the form. *Command:* `shape` / `onboard`

**[P1] WhatsApp link is broken.** `https://wa.me/?text=${...}` (App.tsx:273) has no recipient number  -  opens WhatsApp addressed to nobody. *Why it matters:* this is the explicit low-commitment escape hatch for scared users, and it dead-ends. *Fix:* insert the real business number. *Command:* `harden`

**[P1] Contrast failures on the gold accents.** white-on-gold price pill ≈ 2.3:1 (fails 3:1); eyebrow gold-deep on cream ≈ 3.95:1 at 0.78rem (fails 4.5:1); gold-deep on soft-gold badges ≈ 3.6:1; unstyled placeholders. *Note:* --muted on paper (~5.6:1) and white-0.72 on navy (~9.6:1) both PASS  -  spend the budget on the gold. *Fix:* darken pill text to navy / deepen gold; enlarge or darken eyebrow; set an accessible placeholder color. *Command:* `colorize` / `audit`

**[P1] Form is too long and unreassured for the target user.** 9 fields / 5 required, no payment reassurance, no price echo at package select, package decision unsupported (identical feature lists). *Why it matters:* anxious mobile ESL users abandon long unexplained forms at the commitment point. *Fix:* trim/stage fields, differentiate the packages meaningfully, show selected-package price beside the dropdown, add "no payment now" microcopy. *Command:* `distill` / `clarify`

**[P2] AI-template tells.** Cream default bg (forbidden), 4x eyebrow, twin identical grids, uniform heavy weights. *Fix:* re-ground palette off pure cream, cut eyebrows to <=1, vary weight for real hierarchy, differentiate the grids. *Command:* `quieter` / `typeset`

**Anxious diaspora renter (project persona):** The core fear  -  being scammed  -  is never directly disarmed at the form. No vetting, no faces, no testimonials, no refund/escrow note, no "we never collect your rent." "Let the owner follow up" reads as a one-person operation; "stored in Supabase" reads as a hobby project. This persona bounces exactly when the page needed to close.

## Minor Observations

- Logo image appears 3x (header + two medallions)  -  repetitive; rotated flyer.jpg "preview" risks reading as clip-art/cheap.
- Avenir Next is rarely installed outside Apple; with `font-synthesis: none`, most users fall back to Segoe UI  -  generic, undermining "premium." Self-host the intended typeface.
- h1 `line-height: 0.94` can clip descenders/diacritics  -  risky for ESL names/place-names.
- `--muted` on paper passes (~5.6:1)  -  don't "fix" what isn't broken; spend the contrast budget on the gold pills and eyebrow.

## Questions to Consider

1. If you deleted the word "Supabase," every icon-card grid, and three of four eyebrows, what would be *left* that proves to a scammed renter you're real  -  and why isn't that the hero?
2. Your two packages share identical feature lists. If you can't articulate why Premium exists beyond "5 vs 2 visits," why is the user making that decision at all instead of you recommending one?
3. The most reassuring thing for a scared renter is a real scout's face and a real sample report  -  neither appears anywhere. For a "people-powered" service, where are the people?

# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Diaspora renters  -  people living abroad (Canada, UK, US, EU and elsewhere) who need
to rent a home in their home country or a destination city they cannot physically
visit. They are often arranging a move or securing housing for family from a
distance, on mobile, sometimes over uneven connectivity, and frequently reading in
English as a second language.

Their context is high-stakes and anxious: they are about to send money or sign
documents for a property they have never seen, and rental scams are a real and
present fear. The job to be done is **"let me verify this home is real and right
before I commit"**  -  get trustworthy, on-the-ground evidence (photos, video, red-flag
checks, area notes, a debrief call) from a local scout, then submit a request to get
that scout assigned.

## Product Purpose

Online Scout is a human-powered home-verification service. A local scout visits a
property on the renter's behalf, documents it honestly, checks for red flags, and
reports back so the renter can decide from anywhere. The site exists to (1) earn the
trust of someone who has been burned or fears being burned, and (2) convert that
trust into a submitted scout request.

Success is a qualified scout request  -  but trust is the gate that request passes
through. A visitor who believes the service is real, careful, and on their side is
the whole game; the form is just where that belief gets recorded. Secondary success
is a started conversation on WhatsApp or email.

## Brand Personality

Confident, modern, premium  -  but grounded in trust, never flashy. The voice is calm,
direct, and plain-spoken: it makes specific promises ("a local scout checks the
property before you send money") rather than hype. It should feel like a real,
competent service run by people who understand exactly how frightening a
long-distance rental is, and who have your back on the ground.

Emotional goals: **relief, safety, and confidence.** The renter should exhale  - 
"finally, someone I can trust to actually go look." Premium here means precise and
considered, not loud or decorated.

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI/SaaS template look.** No cream-default backgrounds, no tiny uppercase
  tracked eyebrows on every section, no endless identical icon-card grids. If it
  could be any startup, it's wrong.
- **Sketchy / scammy / cheap.** The single most damaging failure for this audience.
  No clip-art, no clutter, no hard-sell urgency theatrics, nothing that reads as
  fly-by-night. Every pixel must earn trust.
- **Cold corporate / sterile.** No faceless enterprise feel. This is a people-powered
  local service; warmth and humanity must come through.
- **Loud / flashy / overdesigned.** No heavy gradients, neon, gradient text, or
  aggressive motion. Confidence is shown through restraint and craft, not volume.

## Design Principles

- **Trust is the product.** Every section either provides evidence of credibility or
  removes a reason to doubt. When a choice trades polish for believability,
  believability wins.
- **Show the work, don't just claim it.** Concrete proof over adjectives  -  real
  photos/video, the actual checklist a scout runs, named deliverables, transparent
  pricing. "Honest insight" is demonstrated, not asserted.
- **Plain language, no jargon.** Copy reads at a glance for an ESL, first-time, often
  mobile user. Short sentences, specific promises, zero marketing fog.
- **Calm confidence through restraint.** Premium is achieved by precision, spacing,
  typography, and a committed palette  -  not by decoration. Quiet beats loud.
- **Reduce the fear of committing.** The path from "I'm scared to send money" to "I
  submitted a request" should feel safe at every step: clear next actions, no
  pressure, easy human contact (WhatsApp/email) as a low-commitment on-ramp.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA, with extra emphasis on plain-language clarity for ESL and
first-time users.

- Body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast (large text ≥ 3:1), including placeholders and any text
  on the gold/navy fields. Mind muted gray on tinted backgrounds.
- Mobile-first and resilient on slower connections  -  the audience is predominantly on
  phones, sometimes with uneven bandwidth.
- Full keyboard operability and visible focus states; the request form labelled and
  screen-reader friendly with clear, specific error and success messaging.
- Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` with a non-motion alternative for every animation.
- Don't rely on color alone to convey meaning (e.g. form status pairs color with an
  icon and text).
